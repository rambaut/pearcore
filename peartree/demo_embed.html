Background

The 2014–2016 West African Ebola virus disease (EVD) outbreak was the largest and most complex Ebola outbreak since the virus was first discovered in 1976. The epidemic affected mainly Guinea, Liberia, and Sierra Leone, with over 28,000 reported cases and 11,000 deaths.

Whole-genome sequencing of viral isolates throughout the outbreak enabled real-time phylogenetic analysis, revealing a single introduction into the human population from a zoonotic reservoir, followed by sustained human-to-human transmission and geographic spread. The tree below illustrates the evolutionary relationships among the sequenced EBOV genomes.
